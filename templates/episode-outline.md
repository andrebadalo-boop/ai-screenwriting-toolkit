# Episode Outline — Template

> An outline is the **skeleton with nerves**: every block must have a dramatic
> function and end on a turn. If a block only "conveys information", it is not
> a block — fold it into one that moves. Fill each field; delete *italics*.

## Episode header
- **Series & season:**
- **Episode number & title:**
- **One-line episode logline:** *this episode's own mini-arc, in one sentence.*
- **How this episode serves the SERIES ENGINE:** *one line — which recurring
  machine of conflict does this episode advance or complicate?*

## Storylines (keep them honest)
- **A-story (plot engine):** *the external line that drives the episode.*
- **B-story (emotional / theme):** *the relationship line that carries meaning.*
- **C-story (runner / world):** *optional — a smaller line that pays off later.*
> Rule: the A and B stories must **cross** at least once in the episode.
> If they never touch, you have two short films stapled together.

## The end-of-episode hook (the reason to press "next")
*One line. This is not a recap — it is a **new question** or a **new threat**
that the episode's resolution has just opened. The best hooks reframe what we
thought we were watching.*
