# AI Screenwriting Toolkit 🎬

An **open, AI-assisted screenwriting toolkit**: battle-tested prompts, reusable
templates and end-to-end workflows for writers, directors and film students.
Works with **Claude** or any large language model.

> Built in the open, free to reuse, remix and teach with.
> If it helps you, please share it back.

## Why this exists

Most "AI for writing" content online is generic. This toolkit is different:
it encodes the **actual craft workflow** of a working writer-director —
from logline to pitch deck — into prompts and templates that anyone can run.

The goal is not to replace the writer. It is to give independent, low-budget
and non-English-language creators a **reproducible pipeline** for research,
structure, character, rewriting and pitching — the same pipeline taught in
the classroom and used on internationally awarded productions.

## Who it's for

- ️ **Screenwriters** developing features or series
- 🎥 **Writer-directors** building pitch materials for funding & co-production
- 🎓 **Film students & teachers** who want a structured, shareable method
- 🌍 **Independent creators** working outside the big-studio system

## What's inside

| Folder | Contents |
|---|---|
| `prompts/` | Copy-paste prompts for each stage of development (logline → pitch) |
| `templates/` | Blank, fill-in structures (synopsis, series bible, episode outline) |
| `workflows/` | Step-by-step pipelines that chain prompts together |

### Prompts
1. **Logline & premise** — pressure-test the core idea in one sentence
2. **Character bible** — build characters with want / need / wound / arc
3. **Beat sheet & structure** — break the story into acts and beats
4. **Scene & dialogue rewrite** — sharpen a scene for subtext and pace
5. **Pitch deck & treatment** — turn the script into sellable materials

### Templates
- One-page synopsis
- Series bible
- Episode outline

### Workflows
- **Research → first draft** — a full pipeline from blank page to draft

## How to use it (with Claude or any LLM)

1. Open any file in `prompts/`.
2. Copy the block inside the ` ``` ` fences.
3. Paste it into Claude (or your model of choice).
4. Replace the `{{VARIABLES}}` with your project's details.
5. Iterate. The model is a writing partner, not an oracle — **you decide**.

> Tip: feed the model your existing material first (a synopsis, a scene,
> a character note) *before* the prompt, so it works from your voice.

## Principles

- **Craft first, AI second.** The prompts encode screenwriting craft; the
  model just executes it faster.
- **Voice is yours.** Always rewrite the output in your own language.
- **Open by default.** Reuse, remix, translate, teach — just keep attribution.
- **Built for the classroom.** Designed to be handed to students as-is.
