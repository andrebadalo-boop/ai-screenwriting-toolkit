# 02 — Character Bible

## Objective
Build a three-dimensional character with a clear **want**, **need**, **wound**
and **arc** — the engine that drives every scene they are in.

## When to use
At development stage, before or during the first draft, for any main character
or antagonist. Run it once per character.

## The prompt
```
You are a script consultant working with a professional screenwriter.
I will give you a character sketch. Your job is NOT to write the script.
Your job is to pressure-test and deepen the character using the framework
below. Be specific, concrete and unsentimental. Challenge weak choices.

Return the character bible in this exact structure:

1. ONE-LINE ESSENCE — who they are in a single, vivid line.
2. WANT (external goal) — the concrete, visible thing they chase.
3. NEED (internal truth) — what they actually need to learn/accept.
4. WOUND / GHOST — the past event that created their false belief.
5. LIE THEY BELIEVE — the wrong worldview driving their bad decisions.
6. FLAW IN ACTION — how the flaw shows behaviourally (not adjectives).
7. MASK vs SELF — who they pretend to be vs who they are.
8. ARC — start state → midpoint shift → end state (be specific).
9. CONTRADICTION — one humanizing contradiction that makes them real.
10. VOICE — 3 lines of sample dialogue that sound ONLY like them.

After the bible, add a short section:
- SCENE TEST: propose ONE scene that would force this character to confront
  their lie.

Here is my character sketch:
{{CHARACTER_SKETCH}}

Genre and tone of the project: {{GENRE_TONE}}
```

## Variables
- `{{CHARACTER_SKETCH}}` — your rough notes (a paragraph is enough).
- `{{GENRE_TONE}}` — e.g. “historical drama, restrained, 1970s Portugal”.

## Example input
> *A 40-year-old censor in a dictatorship who secretly loves forbidden books.
> Project: historical drama about the last literary trial of the regime.*

## Notes
- The **want vs need** gap is where your story lives. If they match, push harder.
- A character without a **contradiction** reads as a poster, not a person.
- Re-run after drafting: the character often reveals their true self on the page.
